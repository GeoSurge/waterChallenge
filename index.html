Lead Levels in California Schools

There is no safe level of lead, especially for children. Even small amounts can lower IQ. Damage from lead exposure is irreversible.

California community water systems are required by the state to test drinking water for lead at the schools they serve by June 2019.

This map shows the highest lead levels detected at each school based on the latest publicly available data as of 3/12/2019. It includes data through 1/12/2019. In cases where school districts have provided more data, we included that too.

Although there is no safe level of lead, the state is only required to share results when lead levels are above 5 PPB. Some schools did their own testing and have publicly shared results down to 1PPB. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends no more than 1 PPB in drinking water for children.
